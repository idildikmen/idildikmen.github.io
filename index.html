What is the problem?

With the widespread integration of AI technologies, facial recognition systems have gained importance in various aspects of our lives, from security and authentication to entertainment and social media. However, these systems are shown to be biased against some demographic groups. Most of the image datasets that are currently used for building facial recognition technologies mainly contain images of middle-aged light-skinned males. No wonder our systems are biased!

This problem goes beyond facial recognition and extends into other technologies reliant on facial images. For instance, deepfake detection is designed to distinguish real content from synthetic media and makes use of facial images. Similarly, avatar creation and computer vision technologies are new innovations where facial images are used. Facial recognition systems analyze the unique features of an individual's face and translate them into life-like avatars. The avatar technology goes beyond just visual replication but is capable of capturing expressions and emotions. This enables users the ability to interact and communicate with a deeper sense of connection and relatability.

Computer vision focuses on teaching machines to interpret and understand visual data as humans do. In this context, facial images are fundamental for training algorithms to recognize and differentiate individual faces. If these systems are not trained with a wide variety of facial images, they might end up continuing to perpetuate bias. As we navigate the potential of AI to shape our future, addressing these biases becomes a priority to ensure we have fairness, equity, and accuracy across all dimensions of AI-enabled technologies.

Our Effort to Improve Fairness

Several current facial image datasets, not only lack diversity in important aspects such as age, gender, race/ethnicity, and skin tone, but they are also often not ethically sourced. As a group of scientists who work on ethical AI, we want to build the largest ethically sourced and diverse facial image dataset with your help. We ask you to self-identify your demographical attributes and upload 7 facial images to our system every two months, maintaining this routine within a 6-month interval. By continuously integrating new images, AI systems can adapt their recognition algorithms to the evolving nature of human faces, ultimately leading to enhanced accuracy, robustness, and performance. Moreover, this iterative process minimizes biases stemming from outdated data and contributes to the ethical and fair deployment of facial recognition technology. What makes this dataset ethcially sourced is because people like you decide if you want to join. Your partipation is completely voluntary and at any point, you can decide to opt-out. Therefore, the control lies within your hands, providing you a sense of automony that other datasets do not provide.

Data Collection and Privacy Protocols

The data for our collection includes facial images and demographic information such as age, gender, and ethnicity. Contact data such as name and e-mail address are stored separately and will only be used to follow up with participants. Contact data will be deleted after the termination of the project. The data will be stored for at least 10 years and will be provided only for research purposes within the rules of the Max Planck Society. You have the right to withdraw from the “DIVERSE FACES DATASET COLLECTION” at any time. You can see more at “Legal and Ethical Terms”.
